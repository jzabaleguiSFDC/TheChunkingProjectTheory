The Theory

Every implementation firm wants 10× with AI. The critical path says otherwise — it belongs to the client, not the implementer.

Mirrors animation 1 · intro
salesforce.01 · The Theory
The premise & the thesis

The 10× dream meets the client's pace

1 year → 1 month
The industry dream. This briefing argues that, in the general case, that compression is not possible — and explains why.
  • The critical path is set by dependencies on the client, not by how fast the implementer works.
  • AI accelerates the implementer's side; it does not automatically accelerate the client's ability to keep pace.
salesforce.01 · The Theory
Mechanism & corollary

Asymmetric acceleration — so shrink the team, or it's impossible

~3 weeks → ~1 week
Internal work collapses on the implementer's side — review and tests included — but the client won't necessarily give feedback every week instead of every 3 weeks. (Illustrative, not a benchmark.)
1

Keep chunk size, shrink the team

Size the team to the client's real pace — not the implementer's possible pace.

2

Compression is impossible

The critical path sits outside the implementer's control. AI only changes who the bottleneck is.

Shrink the boxes as much as the industry dreams — even the famous 10× — and the project becomes made mostly of gates. When the work is tiny, the gates dominate the timeline.

And the smaller box grows governance: AI produces more, so there's more to review — technical review, end-to-end tests, quality. Speed only counts if that rides inside.

Shrinking boxes vs. moving gates

Three initiatives, two axes

Offer side

SEAP

A multitool that plays roles and saves to memory — aspires to shrink many boxes at once. Still only shrinks boxes.

The default

AI Delivery

Boxes shrink, gates stay. Freed capacity becomes more scope or quality — it changes what you deliver, not when.

Client + offer

Quantum Leap

The only lever that moves the gates — a bilateral, contractual commitment on pace from both sides.

Architects Mastering AI

Master the Fluency, know the Assets — cut through the noise, and train the muscle to see the use case.

Mirrors animation 4 · architects mastering AI
salesforce.04 · Architects Mastering AI
Two levers, two efforts

Master the Fluency, know the Assets

Human skill — MASTER

AI Fluency

Basic AI, and knowing when to use it — like learning Excel. Two things: the jargon, and reshaping how you think (the real muscle — developed, not copied).

Packaged — KNOW

AI Assets

Complete, end-to-end solutions — Setup Agents, Slackbot, LucidAI, OAT. Know which one fits which chunk; you don't rebuild them.

Signal vs. noise

Noise is inevitable — telling it apart is fluency

The noise

Everyone's eager to share

Wins, learnings, tools — well-meaning, but the volume buries the signal and architects lose focus on what matters. It can't be switched off.

The signal — AI Pills

Curated vitamins, 100% valid

  • Share — a Slack workflow: how AI was used + how to reproduce it.
  • Socialize — weekly Friday sessions on real use cases.
  • Embed — every KSO asset must use AI (e.g. OAT).

Pills don't remove the effort — time, install, research — they help it develop.

salesforce.04 · Architects Mastering AI
The subtlest pill

Understand the use case — don't paste the work

A skill

Very useful — but not an asset to copy-paste. It exists to open your mind to a use case you could have solved yourself.

Where value lives
  • Reuse lives in the AI Asset.
  • The muscle lives in AI Fluency.
  • Confuse them, and you never train fluency.

The goal isn't reusing someone's work — it's building the muscle to see the use case.

salesforce.04 · Architects Mastering AI
For management

What to do with this

  • Size teams to the client's pace, not the implementer's — the big compression is real inside chunks, not at project level (10× is the industry's aspiration; the real factor is usually a few times).
  • Invest in AI Fluency as a trained muscle (AI Pills, signal over noise) and in AI Assets as reuse — two different investments.
  • To compress at project level, pursue Quantum Leap: contractual, bilateral gate commitments. Everything else optimizes the side that was never the bottleneck.
