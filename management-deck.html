Executive Briefing

The Chunking Project Theory

Why AI does not turn a one-year implementation into a one-month project — and where the real acceleration lever actually is.

Salesforce Professional Services · Iberia
salesforce.Executive Briefing
The premise

Every implementation firm wants 10× with AI

1 year → 1 month
The industry dream: compress a full delivery into a fraction of the time by pointing AI at the work.

This briefing argues that, in the general case, that compression is not possible — and explains precisely why.

salesforce.
Central thesis

The critical path belongs to the client, not the implementer

  • A project's critical path is not set by how fast the implementer works — it is set by the dependencies on the client.
  • AI accelerates the implementer's side. It does not automatically accelerate the client's ability to keep pace.
  • Speeding up the side that isn't on the critical path does not compress the project.
salesforce.
Key concept

A project splits into chunks between client dependencies

Type

Each chunk has a type driven by project phase and project kind — and needs its own AI tools.

Tools

Discovery leans on transcription & docs; build leans on Cursor, Claude Code, Codex. Different chunk, different stack.

Boundaries

Every chunk starts and/or ends where the client is needed — feedback, a meeting, requirements, an approval.

  • Both gates are opened by the client. AI never opens a gate — it only speeds the work between them.
  • The gates are the project's inflection points: they are exactly the client dependencies, and the critical path is drawn gate to gate.
  • A blocked chunk has its entry gate shut — classic cases: API specs for integrations, clean data for migration.

The human in the loop

The integrator drives the AI and works the gates

Inside the box

Drives & supervises the AI

AI accelerates the internal work but doesn't decide alone. Someone directs it, reviews the output, and owns the result. No driver — no controlled compression.

At the gates

Helps the client open on time

The most critical role: prepare the artifact ready to decide, chase the missing input, cut the latency of every dependency. The human moves the project forward — not the AI.

The mechanism

Asymmetric acceleration

3 weeks → 3 days
A classic sprint's internal work collapses on the implementer's side.

But the client who gave feedback, held meetings and verbalized requirements every 3 weeks is not guaranteed to do it every 3 days. The project compresses only as far as the client allows.

salesforce.
Corollary

Shrink the team — or accept it's impossible

1

Keep chunk size, shrink the team

If the client can only close dependencies every 3 weeks, a team sized to produce every 3 days is over-provisioned. Size the team to the client's real pace.

2

Compression is impossible

The 1-year-in-1-month goal fails, because the critical path sits outside the implementer's control.

AI doesn't remove the client's critical path — it only changes who the bottleneck is.

Two acceleration levers

AI Fluency and AI Assets — both shrink the box

Human skill

AI Fluency

People using basic AI and knowing when to use it — like learning Excel twenty years ago. A base literacy, not a product.

Packaged solution

AI Assets

Complete, end-to-end solutions that accelerate a chunk — Setup Agents, Slackbot, LucidAI, OAT. One abstraction level above fluency.

Both mainly shrink the box; at best they trim gate latency — they never open the gate. That's still the client's.

The subtlest pill

Understand the use case — don't paste the work

A skill

Very useful — but not an asset to copy-paste. An asset is self-contained and elaborate; a skill exists to open your mind to a use case you could have solved yourself.

Where value lives
  • Reuse lives in the AI Asset.
  • The muscle lives in AI Fluency.
  • Confuse them, and you never train fluency.

The goal isn't reusing someone's work — it's building the muscle to see the use case.

Fit inside Salesforce

Shrinking boxes vs. moving gates

Offer side

SEAP

A multitool that plays roles and saves to memory — aspires to shrink many boxes at once. Still only shrinks boxes.

Default

AI Delivery

Boxes shrink, gates stay. Freed capacity turns into more scope or more quality — it changes what you deliver, not when.

Client + offer

Quantum Leap

The only lever that moves the gates — a bilateral, contractual commitment on pace from both sides. The only one that touches the critical path.

For management

What to do with this

  • The "10×" is real inside chunks — not at project level. Size teams to the client's pace, not the implementer's.
  • Invest in AI Fluency as a trained muscle (AI Pills), and in AI Assets as reuse — they are different investments.
  • To compress at project level, pursue Quantum Leap: contractual, bilateral gate commitments. Everything else optimizes the side that was never the bottleneck.
